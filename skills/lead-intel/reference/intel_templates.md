# Intel Templates & Quality Guidelines

## Pain Signal — SMB (Question Format)

### Template
```
Are you still [specific painful process] when [your solution benefit]?
```

### Good Examples
- "Are you still manually researching prospects on LinkedIn when you could have AI surface your best-fit leads automatically?"
- "Are you still losing deals because your SDRs can't personalize at scale?"
- "Is your team spending 3+ hours per day on prospect research instead of actual selling?"

### Bad Examples (avoid)
- "Are you looking for a better solution?" (too vague)
- "Do you want to save money?" (generic, not role-specific)
- "Have you heard about AI for sales?" (product-centric, not pain-centric)

### Rules
- Must reference a **specific** pain point relevant to their **title** and **industry**
- Must imply a better way without hard-selling
- Keep under 150 characters
- Use "you/your" language, not "we/our"

## Pain Signal — Mid/Enterprise (Statement Format)

### Template
```
[Their role] at [their company type] typically struggle with [specific challenge], especially when [contextual detail from research]. This creates [business impact].
```

### Good Example
"VPs of Marketing at mid-size SaaS companies typically struggle with attribution across increasingly complex multi-touch funnels, especially as they scale paid channels beyond Google. This creates misallocated budget and makes it hard to justify headcount to the CFO."

## Lead Intel (Mid/Enterprise)

### Template
```
[Personalized observation about their situation]. [Connection to a trend or signal]. [Bridge to how this relates to what you offer — without naming your product].
```

### Good Example
"Acme Corp's recent expansion into EMEA (announced Q1) means their current US-centric tech stack may not scale for multi-region compliance. With GDPR enforcement increasing, their engineering team is likely evaluating infrastructure that can handle data residency requirements."

### Rules
- 2-3 sentences max
- Reference specific, verifiable facts about the company
- Never mention your product by name
- Focus on their situation, not your pitch

## Email Subject Lines (Mid/Enterprise)

### Rules
- Under 60 characters
- No ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- No clickbait or misleading content
- Personalize with company name or role when possible
- Question format performs well

### Good Examples
- "Quick question about Acme's EMEA expansion"
- "[First name], re: your team's hiring push"
- "Idea for {company}'s Q3 pipeline goal"

## Email Opening (Mid/Enterprise)

### Template
```
[Personalized hook — reference something specific about them/their company].
[Bridge sentence connecting their situation to a relevant insight].
```

### Good Example
"Noticed Acme just opened a London office — congrats on the expansion. Curious if your team has thought through how your current prospecting workflow scales across time zones."

### Rules
- Never start with "I" or "My name is"
- Never start with "I hope this email finds you well"
- Reference something specific and verifiable
- Keep to 2 sentences max

## Trigger Detail (Enterprise Only)

### Template
```
[Specific event] + [when it happened] + [why it matters for outreach timing]
```

### Good Example
"Acme appointed a new CTO (Sarah Chen, ex-Stripe) 6 weeks ago. New tech leaders typically evaluate and replace incumbent vendors within their first 90 days, creating a window for alternative solutions."

## Email CTA (Enterprise Only)

### Rules
- Match to their authority level:
  - C-suite: Strategic conversation, peer introduction
  - VP: 15-min briefing, custom analysis
  - Director: Demo, case study from similar company
- Low commitment, high value
- Specific time suggestion

### Good Example (C-suite)
"Would it be worth a 15-minute call to share how [similar company] approached this? Happy to work around your schedule next week."
